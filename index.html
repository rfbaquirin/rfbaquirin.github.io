My process

How I approach every brand

Every listing I touch starts with diagnosis, not design. Understanding why a listing underperforms is the work — the visuals are the output.

01

Audit

Review all current assets — listing images, copy, A+, storefront — against Amazon's content standards and category benchmarks.

02

Benchmark

Identify top competitors and category leaders. Map what's working — visual hierarchy, trust signals, lifestyle approach — against your current gaps.

03

Strategy

Define the creative direction — tone, visual hierarchy, messaging priorities, trust signals — before a single image is designed.

04

Design

Execute the full image stack — main image, infographics, lifestyle, A+, EBC — with AI tools integrated for speed without sacrificing craft.

05

Optimize

Monitor performance, iterate on underperforming images, and roll out improvements systematically across Tier-A ASINs.

Case Study 01

Lure Minerals Foundation
— Advanced Skin Care

A premium mineral beauty brand with generic Amazon visuals. Low trust signals, weak conversion confidence, and messaging that didn't match category leaders.

Listing Redesign A+ Content Competitive Audit Beauty / Cosmetics 90-Day Sprint

Gaps identified

  • Visuals lacked premium beauty positioning — felt mass-market
  • Messaging was feature-heavy with no emotional connection
  • Limited trust-building vs. category leaders like BareMinerals
  • No skin-confidence storytelling in lifestyle imagery
  • A+ content lacked modern clean-beauty structure and hierarchy

Direction taken

Competitive benchmark

The category leader sets the standard — strong trust positioning, clinical and emotional balance, inclusive shade representation. Lure needed to meet that bar with a warmer, more aspirational feel.

Customer review mining

5-star themes: lightweight coverage, natural finish, skin-friendly. 1-star pain points: cakey look, shade uncertainty. Creative direction focused on reducing hesitation and building shade confidence.

Creative strategy

Softer luxury aesthetic · Cleaner visual hierarchy · Real-skin lifestyle imagery · Stronger educational flow · Improved trust communication throughout the listing journey.

Case Study 02

Sleep Fountain
— Copper-Infused Sleep Mask

A sleep mask with a genuine material differentiator — copper-infused interior fabric — that was being over-emphasized in ways that violated Amazon's medical claims policy. The challenge wasn't invisibility. It was repositioning copper from a medical claim to a credible wellness advantage.

Full Listing Audit Image Stack Rebuild Competitive Analysis Sleep / Wellness CTR & CVR Strategy

Gaps identified

  • Copper-infused interior was being communicated with medical-style claims not permitted on Amazon
  • Language like "boosts collagen" and "reduces wrinkles" created policy risk and undermined credibility
  • No clear answer to "why choose this over others?" beyond the copper claim
  • Main image technically correct but not differentiated enough in a crowded SERP
  • Images explained the product but didn't prove performance or remove purchase doubt
  • Key objections — light leakage, slipping, skin irritation — not addressed in the listing flow

Direction taken

Competitive benchmark

The category is visually saturated — most masks look identical. Customers decide based on perceived comfort, fit clarity, and trust signals. No competitor owned a skin-friendly positioning, but owning it required compliant language.

Repositioning strategy

Reframed copper from a medical claim to a wellness differentiator. Shifted language from "boosts collagen / reduces wrinkles" to "designed for skin-friendly sleep / reduces friction during wear." Same advantage, compliant positioning, stronger credibility.

Image stack rebuild

Rebuilt the 9-image sequence with a clear job: Stop the scroll → Explain the value → Remove doubt. Each image assigned a single role: blackout proof, fit, copper wellness framing, comfort, stability, use case, comparison, objection handling.

Listing image transformation

Copper advantage over-communicated with medical-style claims — policy risk on Amazon. Visually flat with no scroll-stopping differentiation in a crowded SERP.

Copper repositioned as a wellness differentiator — skin-friendly, friction-reducing language that's compliant and credible. Cleaner visual hierarchy with a stronger reason to click.

Case Study 03

Benson's Gourmet Seasoning
— Table Tasty Salt Substitute

A sodium-free seasoning with strong trust signals and credible health positioning — but listing images that explained the product without selling it. The A+ content told a compelling story. The image gallery didn't match.

Listing Redesign A+ Content Competitive Audit Health / Grocery Conversion Strategy

Gaps identified

  • Listing images were educational but not emotional — explained the product without selling the experience
  • No flavor visualization — the #1 customer anxiety ("will this actually taste good?") was not addressed in the image stack
  • Lifestyle integration was low — no everyday usage context, no family meal scenarios
  • Social proof was minimal — a product with strong reviews wasn't surfacing them in the gallery
  • A+ content had been rebuilt to best-in-category level, creating a disconnect: strong emotional story in A+, average merchandising in the image stack

Direction taken

Category insight

Customers in this category aren't buying a seasoning — they're buying flavor freedom. The winning emotional trigger across all competitor reviews: "I can eat the foods I love again." Bitterness is the category's biggest fear. Taste satisfaction drives loyalty more than health claims alone.

Positioning strategy

Benson's already occupied the strongest territory in the category: "Healthy Food Without Sacrifice" — combining health, flavor, trust, and lifestyle without going clinical. The rebuild aligned the image gallery to that same emotional promise: enjoy the foods you love, flavor without compromise.

Image stack rebuild

Rebuilt the gallery to close the gap with the A+ content. Each image assigned a specific conversion job: appetite appeal up front, flavor confidence mid-stack, trust signals and social proof at close. The goal: same emotional story in both gallery and A+, from first scroll to purchase decision.
