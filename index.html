My process

How I approach every brand

Every listing I touch starts with diagnosis, not design. Understanding why a listing underperforms is the work — the visuals are the output.

01

Audit

Review all current assets — listing images, copy, A+, storefront — against Amazon's content standards and category benchmarks.

02

Benchmark

Identify top competitors and category leaders. Map what's working — visual hierarchy, trust signals, lifestyle approach — against your current gaps.

03

Strategy

Define the creative direction — tone, visual hierarchy, messaging priorities, trust signals — before a single image is designed.

04

Design

Execute the full image stack — main image, infographics, lifestyle, A+, EBC — with AI tools integrated for speed without sacrificing craft.

05

Optimize

Monitor performance, iterate on underperforming images, and roll out improvements systematically across Tier-A ASINs.

Case Study 01

Lure Minerals Foundation
— Advanced Skin Care

A premium mineral beauty brand with generic Amazon visuals. Low trust signals, weak conversion confidence, and messaging that didn't match category leaders.

Listing Redesign A+ Content Competitive Audit Beauty / Cosmetics 90-Day Sprint

Gaps identified

  • Visuals lacked premium beauty positioning — felt mass-market
  • Messaging was feature-heavy with no emotional connection
  • Limited trust-building vs. category leaders like BareMinerals
  • No skin-confidence storytelling in lifestyle imagery
  • A+ content lacked modern clean-beauty structure and hierarchy

Direction taken

Competitive benchmark

The category leader sets the standard — strong trust positioning, clinical and emotional balance, inclusive shade representation. Lure needed to meet that bar with a warmer, more aspirational feel.

Customer review mining

5-star themes: lightweight coverage, natural finish, skin-friendly. 1-star pain points: cakey look, shade uncertainty. Creative direction focused on reducing hesitation and building shade confidence.

Creative strategy

Softer luxury aesthetic · Cleaner visual hierarchy · Real-skin lifestyle imagery · Stronger educational flow · Improved trust communication throughout the listing journey.

Case Study 02

Sleep Fountain
— Copper-Infused Sleep Mask

A sleep mask with a genuine material advantage — copper-infused interior fabric — that was being treated as a footnote. The product wasn't underperforming because of what it was. It was underperforming because its strongest differentiator wasn't visible anywhere in the listing.

Full Listing Audit Image Stack Rebuild Competitive Analysis Sleep / Wellness CTR & CVR Strategy

Gaps identified

  • No clear answer to "why choose this over the others?" — listing blended into a crowded SERP
  • Copper-infused interior treated as a secondary feature when it should anchor the entire positioning
  • Main image technically correct but strategically weak — no visual differentiation at scroll speed
  • Images explained the product but didn't prove performance or remove doubt
  • Key objections — light leakage, slipping, skin irritation — not addressed in the listing flow
  • A+ content reinforced the product but didn't create a compelling reason to choose it

Direction taken

Category insight

The SERP is visually saturated — most masks look identical. Customers scan shape first and make decisions on perceived comfort, fit clarity, and trust signals. No competitor owned skin-friendly positioning, leaving a clear white space.

Repositioning strategy

Moved Sleep Fountain out of the "silk mask" commodity category and into a new position: a performance sleep mask designed for both better sleep and better skin. Copper becomes the proof point, not a spec.

Image stack rebuild

Rebuilt the 9-image sequence around a clear job: Stop the scroll (CTR) → Explain the value instantly → Remove doubt (CVR). Each image assigned a single job: blackout proof, fit, copper differentiator, comfort, stability, use case, comparison, objection handling.
